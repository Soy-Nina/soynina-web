Gender-based violence is a human rights crisis that begins early. At Soy Niña, we believe that prevention from childhood is key to breaking cycles of violence and building equitable relationships.

## Concerning Data

In Costa Rica, as in many countries, gender-based violence figures are alarming. But what is frequently overlooked is that patterns of violence begin in childhood and adolescence:

- 1 in 3 adolescents experiences relationship violence
- Violence starts with micro-aggressions and controlling behaviors
- Many girls normalize violence because they don't know they can expect something different

## Our Prevention Approach

**Rights Education:** We teach girls and adolescents about their fundamental rights, including the right to live free from violence.

**Signal Identification:** We help recognize behaviors of control, manipulation, and abuse from an early age.

**Skills Development:** We strengthen assertive communication, boundary setting, and conflict resolution.

**Support Network:** We create safe spaces where girls can talk and receive guidance.

## Prevention Through Empowerment

When a girl loves herself, when she knows her worth and her rights, she does not accept violent behavior. When an adolescent is empowered, she has the confidence to say "no" and seek help when she needs it.

Preventing gender-based violence is not only the responsibility of women—all members of society have a role. That is why our programs also work with families, educators, and communities to create safe environments.

## Together We Can Make a Difference

Gender-based violence is not inevitable. Through education, empowerment, and cultural change, we can create a Costa Rica where all girls grow up safe, respected, and free.
