Empowering girls and adolescents is not just a social goal—it is an investment in the future of Costa Rica. When we empower a girl, we empower her family, her community, and ultimately, an entire nation.

## What Does Empowerment Mean?

Empowerment goes beyond simply giving power. It is a comprehensive process that includes:

- **Self-knowledge:** Understanding who we are, our strengths, and our rights
- **Comprehensive Education:** Access to quality education that prepares us for life
- **Voice and Participation:** Opportunities to express ourselves and participate in decisions that affect us
- **Resilience:** The ability to face challenges and recover
- **Autonomy:** The freedom to make decisions about our lives

## Why Focus on Girls?

In many societies, including ours, girls face unique barriers. From gender pressures that limit them from an early age to lack of access to educational and economic opportunities. When we invest in girl empowerment, we are tackling at the root the inequalities that later perpetuate throughout adult life.

## The Multiplier Effect

International research has shown time and again that when a girl is empowered:

- She has better academic performance
- She is less likely to be a victim of violence or abuse
- She develops healthier and more equitable relationships
- She contributes economically to her community
- She is more likely to raise her own children with values of equality
