At Soy Niña, we understand that we cannot empower girls and adolescents without strengthening their families and communities. That is why our Families program is much more than a workshop—it is a community transformation.

## The Context

Many families in vulnerable communities face enormous challenges: poverty, lack of access to quality education, intergenerational patterns of violence, and limited resources. But within that reality, there is infinite potential for change.

## What Makes Our Families Program Special?

Our Families program works on three levels:

**Individual Level:** We strengthen parenting skills, family relationships, and conflict resolution in specific families.

**Community Level:** We create support networks among families facing similar challenges. The community becomes the support system.

**Structural Level:** We work with community leaders, schools, and local authorities to generate broader changes.

## Stories of Resilience

One of our participating mothers, Rosa, came to the program feeling completely alone. She had taken her two daughters out of school because "there was no money." Through the program, Rosa not only improved her economic situation through vocational training, but she rebuilt her relationship with her daughters and became a rights advocate for other mothers in her community.

Today, her daughters are back in school, and Rosa leads a group of 15 mothers in her neighborhood who support each other and advocate together for better opportunities for their families.

## The Multiplier Effect

What is beautiful about working with families is the multiplier effect. An empowered mother empowers her daughters. A family that heals inspires other families. A community that strengthens generates structural change.

150+ families have gone through our Families program. Each one of them is a seed of change planted in their community.

## Future Vision

Our goal is to expand the Families program to more communities in Costa Rica. We want no girl to grow up in a family without support, no mother to feel alone, every community to have the opportunity to build resilience together.

Resilience is not just surviving. It is thriving together, growing together, and transforming together.
